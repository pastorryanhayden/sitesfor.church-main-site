3. Managing the Welcome Page

At sitesfor.church we think the three most important things on your website are:

  • Your contact information.
  • Your service schedule.
  • Your welcome page.

Most visitors to your site are going to be looking for the first two, and we want to funnel them towards the "welcome page" - a special page designed to give first time or would be visitors to your church the information that they need to feel at ease when they visit.

Some of the things that should be on a good welcome page are:

  • A description of what your services are like.
  • Information about your children's ministries and nursery.
  • Something that answers the frequently asked questions visitors have like "what do I wear?", "where do you meet?" etc.

The welcome page base is designed to give you a great starting point for a fantastic welcome page. There are a few things you should know about it:

1. The welcome page has a "banded" design with each row in the database making up a "band."#

You can have as many sections as you want. Each row makes up a section.

2. Each band has an optional image spot. We recommend that you stagger the use of these.#

In most themes - the image will show to the right of the text. It looks great for one or two so long as it's spread out. But won't work for every one.

3. The welcome page will always end with a map to your church. We recommend that your last band have something that references that map and how to get to your church building.#

The map is controled by the "Church Info" > "Location" tab.
